Cinch 14-Day Money-Back Guarantee

The basics:
Cinch offers a 14-day "money-back guarantee." If you decide the car isn’t right for you—whether because of how it looks, drives, or simply because you’ve changed your mind—you can return it within 14 days of delivery or collection.

  • Full refund: Purchase price, £99 admin fee, delivery costs (if any), and add-ons like CinchCare or cinchShield.

  • Free collection: Cinch will pick up the car from your home at no extra cost.

  • No reason required: You don’t need to prove a fault to return.

Key rules:

  • You must notify Cinch by 8 pm on the final day of the 14-day period (if that day is a bank holiday, the next working day applies).

  • After 14 days, the guarantee ends. At that point, only Consumer Rights Act protections apply if the car develops a fault.

  • The vehicle must be in essentially the same condition as delivered—no excessive mileage (often 250+ miles), damage, or unusual wear. Charges may apply otherwise.

What happens in practice:

  • Most returns are straightforward, but Reddit users and reviews note that repeated returns can trigger warnings or bans from future purchases. One customer reported being told after a second return that Cinch would no longer sell to them.

  • Cars are inspected on collection; if there’s damage, dents, scratches, or smells, deductions may be made from your refund.

Why this matters:
The 14-day policy is generous compared to traditional dealers. But it also opens the door to potential “gaming”—using Cinch like a free short-term rental:

  1. Buy a car,

  2. Use it for up to two weeks,

  3. Return it for a full refund.

Safeguards against misuse:

  • Multiple returns may flag your account.

  • Excess mileage/wear charges reduce the refund.

  • Abuse of the scheme can lead to being blocked from buying again.

Legal background:
This policy isn’t just goodwill—UK law (Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013) grants buyers a 14-day right to cancel most online purchases, including cars. Cinch aligns its guarantee with that law.

👉 In short: You really can test a Cinch car for 14 days and hand it back at no cost if you’re not happy—but repeated or abusive use may get you flagged, charged for wear, or blocked entirely.
