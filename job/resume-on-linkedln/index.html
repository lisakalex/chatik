On LinkedIn, you can either view your own CV (resume) if you’ve uploaded it, or download another person’s CV-style profile. Here’s how:

1. View your own CV/resume on LinkedIn

LinkedIn doesn’t automatically create a CV, but you can upload one when applying for jobs. To see it:

  • Go to Jobs (briefcase icon).

  • Click Application Settings.

  • Under Resume, you’ll see the CVs you’ve uploaded. You can view or delete them.

Reference: LinkedIn Help – Manage your resumes

2. View your LinkedIn profile as a CV

If you want to see your LinkedIn profile in a CV-like format:

  • Go to your profile.

  • Click the More button (below your headline).

  • Select Save to PDF.

  • This downloads your profile in a CV-style PDF format.

Reference: LinkedIn Help – Save a profile as a PDF

3. View someone else’s CV on LinkedIn

Other users don’t upload CVs directly for public view (unless they attach one to a post or job application). Usually, their LinkedIn profile itself acts as a CV. You can also:

  • Go to their profile.

  • Click More → Save to PDF (if available), to get a CV-style version.

1. Headline (your job title + value add)

2. Profile Photo & Banner

  • Use a clear, professional photo.

  • Add a banner that matches your field (e.g., cloud, coding, data pipelines).

3. About Section (your summary = CV personal statement)

  • 3–4 short paragraphs.

  • Mention years of experience, key skills, and achievements.

  • End with what you’re looking for (e.g., “I’m open to DevOps roles in cloud-native environments in the UK”).

Example:

DevOps Engineer with 5+ years experience in CI/CD, cloud automation, and infrastructure as code. Skilled in Python, Java, Kubernetes, and AWS. Proven track record of improving deployment pipelines and reducing release times by 40%. Currently seeking opportunities to drive automation and reliability in forward-thinking engineering teams.

4. Experience Section (like CV job history)

For each role:

  • Job Title, Company, Dates.

  • 3–5 bullet points with achievements, not duties. Use action verbs + results.

Example:
✔ Automated CI/CD pipeline with GitHub Actions, reducing release failures by 30%.
✔ Migrated on-prem apps to AWS, cutting infrastructure costs by 25%.
✔ Implemented monitoring with Prometheus + Grafana, improving uptime by 15%.

5. Skills & Endorsements

  • Add at least 30+ relevant skills (DevOps, Kubernetes, Docker, Terraform, Jenkins, Python, AWS, CI/CD).

6. Recommendations

  • Ask colleagues or managers to write you a recommendation. Even 2–3 good ones boost credibility.

7. Open to Work & Keywords

  • Turn on “Open to Work” for recruiters.

  • Use keywords recruiters search for in your industry (CI/CD, DevOps, Cloud, Python, Kubernetes, AWS, Terraform).
